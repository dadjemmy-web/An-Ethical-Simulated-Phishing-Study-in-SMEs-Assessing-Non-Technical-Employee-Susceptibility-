# Ethical Measures Summary

**Study:** An Ethical Simulated Phishing Study: Assessing Behavioral Susceptibility in an SME Context and the Implementation of a Targeted phishing Awareness
**Author:** Jesufemi Dada | May 2026

---

## Governing Standards

This study was conducted in compliance with:

- British Psychological Society Code of Human Research Ethics (BPS, 2021)
- UK General Data Protection Regulation (ICO, 2024)
- Nigeria Data Protection Act (2023)

---

## Measures Implemented

| Measure | Detail |
|---|---|
| Informed consent | All 18 participants confirmed written consent by email before any study activity |
| No institutional impersonation | No real brand, logo, organisation name, or letterhead was used in the simulation email |
| No passwords captured | The credential field was non-functional. No password entered was processed, stored, or transmitted |
| Email addresses encrypted in transit | Any address entered was encrypted. No plaintext PII was stored |
| Immediate ethical disclosure | Participants were redirected to a disclosure page immediately after clicking, confirming the simulation and that no real data was captured |
| Fully anonymous survey | Google Form collected no names, email addresses, or identifying metadata |
| Data deletion | All raw data deleted after analysis in compliance with UK GDPR storage limitation principle |
| Right to withdraw | Communicated to all participants. No consequence for non-participation |

---

## GDPR Compliance

| UK GDPR Principle | How Applied |
|---|---|
| Lawfulness and fairness | Prior informed written consent obtained |
| Purpose limitation | Data collected only for stated research purposes |
| Data minimisation | Only anonymous behavioural responses collected |
| Storage limitation | All raw data deleted after analysis |
| Integrity and confidentiality | Passwords not captured; email entries encrypted in transit |
